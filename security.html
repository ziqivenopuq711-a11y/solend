Security

Security First

Protecting user funds is Solend's highest priority. Every architectural decision, parameter choice, and protocol upgrade is evaluated through a security lens first. Solend has undergone four independent security audits by top-tier firms, maintains an active $1,000,000 bug bounty program, and has never lost user funds to a security incident since launching on mainnet in September 2021.

Security Audit Reports

Solend has been audited by four of the most respected smart contract security firms in the industry. All audit reports are publicly available, and all identified issues have been fully remediated prior to deployment.

Kudelski Security

Q3 2021 — Initial Mainnet Audit

Passed

Kudelski Security conducted the first comprehensive audit of Solend's core lending program before the mainnet launch. The audit covered the supply, borrow, repay, withdraw, and liquidation instruction handlers, as well as the account validation logic, arithmetic operations, and access control checks throughout the codebase.

Findings: 0 Critical, 1 High (resolved), 3 Medium (resolved), 5 Low (resolved)

Neodyme

Q1 2022 — Isolated Pools Audit

Passed

Neodyme, specialists in Solana smart contract security, audited the isolated lending pool feature additions and the flash loan implementation. Their review focused on cross-pool interaction risks, reentrancy vectors in the flash loan callback mechanism, and the correctness of the utilization-based interest rate computation across multiple concurrent pools.

Findings: 0 Critical, 0 High, 2 Medium (resolved), 4 Low (resolved)

OtterSec

Q2 2023 — Full Protocol Re-audit

Passed

OtterSec performed a comprehensive re-audit of the entire Solend protocol including all accumulated code changes since the previous audits. The audit scope included the lending program, governance contracts, oracle integration layer, and the off-chain liquidator reference implementation. OtterSec confirmed the protocol's overall security posture and identified minor improvements.

Findings: 0 Critical, 0 High, 1 Medium (resolved), 3 Low (resolved)

Trail of Bits

Q4 2023 — v2 Architecture Audit

Passed

Trail of Bits, one of the most renowned security research firms globally, audited Solend's v2 architecture changes including the upgraded liquidation engine, enhanced oracle safety checks, and new account state management. Their review included both manual code review and automated static analysis using their proprietary tooling, including symbolic execution and fuzzing campaigns on critical functions.

Findings: 0 Critical, 0 High, 2 Medium (resolved), 2 Low (acknowledged)

Bug Bounty Program

Solend operates a public bug bounty program on Immunefi, offering rewards of up to $1,000,000 for the responsible disclosure of critical vulnerabilities. The program covers the core lending smart contracts, governance contracts, and the official frontend application. All security researchers are welcome to participate.

To be eligible for a bounty, vulnerabilities must be previously unknown, not already reported by another researcher, and disclosed responsibly to the Solend security team before public disclosure. Reports should include a clear proof-of-concept demonstrating the impact and steps to reproduce the vulnerability.

Risk Management Framework

Solend employs a multi-layered approach to risk management, combining conservative parameter settings, oracle security, and economic incentive design to protect users under all market conditions.

Oracle Security

Pyth Network provides high-frequency price feeds from institutional market makers. Solend implements price staleness checks (reject feeds older than 60 seconds), confidence interval checks (reject feeds with excessive uncertainty), and circuit breakers that pause affected markets if prices move more than 20% in a single slot.

Liquidation Buffers

Each asset has conservative collateral factors and liquidation thresholds that create buffer zones between a position's borrow limit and its liquidation point. Liquidators are incentivized with 5% bonuses, ensuring fast liquidations before positions go underwater. The 50% close factor prevents protocol-destabilizing single-liquidation events.

Borrow Limits & Debt Ceilings

Every asset has a maximum borrow limit as a percentage of supplied liquidity, and each isolated pool has a global debt ceiling. These caps prevent any single position or pool from accumulating disproportionate risk that could threaten protocol solvency in extreme market scenarios or coordinated attacks against specific assets.

Protocol Insurance Fund

Solend maintains a protocol-owned insurance fund denominated in USDC and SOL, designed to backstop losses in extreme market scenarios where the normal liquidation mechanism fails to fully cover bad debt. The insurance fund acts as a final layer of protection for liquidity providers, separate from any individual user's position.

The fund grows organically through a portion of protocol fee revenue (currently 10% of all interest collected flows to the insurance fund). The fund balance and allocation are visible on-chain and reported in quarterly governance updates. The community can vote to deploy insurance fund resources for emergency recapitalization or other risk-mitigation purposes.

As a last resort, Solend's governance can also vote to use community treasury SLND tokens to recapitalize the protocol through a debt auction mechanism — similar to MakerDAO's emergency shutdown and recapitalization process. This multi-layer backstop system gives users strong confidence in the protocol's long-term solvency.

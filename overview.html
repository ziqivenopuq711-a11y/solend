Protocol Overview

What is Solend?

Solend is an algorithmic, decentralized protocol for lending and borrowing on Solana. It enables anyone, anywhere in the world to earn interest on their digital assets or to borrow against them — without banks, credit checks, or intermediaries. By leveraging Solana's high-speed, low-cost blockchain infrastructure, Solend makes sophisticated DeFi accessible to both retail users and institutions at a fraction of the cost of Ethereum-based alternatives. The protocol is fully on-chain, non-custodial, and governed by its community through the SLND governance token.

How the Supply & Borrow Mechanism Works

Solend operates on a pool-based lending model. Understanding the core mechanics helps users make informed decisions about their positions.

Interest Rate Model

Interest rates on Solend are determined algorithmically based on market utilization — the ratio of borrowed assets to total supplied assets in each pool. As utilization increases toward 100%, borrow rates rise sharply to incentivize repayment and encourage new supply. This creates a self-balancing market that ensures liquidity is always available for withdrawals.

The interest rate curve has two distinct slopes: a gradual increase below the optimal utilization ratio (typically 70–80%), and a steep jump above it. This kink in the curve ensures the protocol can respond dynamically to demand surges without manual intervention, maintaining system stability automatically.

Collateral Factors & Liquidation

Each asset on Solend has a collateral factor that determines how much can be borrowed against it. For example, SOL may have an 80% collateral factor, meaning $100 of SOL collateral allows up to $80 in borrowing. Stable assets with lower volatility typically receive higher collateral factors, while more volatile assets receive lower ones to protect the protocol.

When a borrower's total borrow value exceeds their collateral value multiplied by the liquidation threshold, their position becomes eligible for liquidation. Liquidators can repay up to 50% of the borrow and receive a liquidation bonus (typically 5%) in collateral. This incentive structure ensures that liquidations happen rapidly, keeping the protocol solvent at all times.

Protocol Architecture

Solend's smart contract architecture is designed for security, upgradeability, and composability — enabling both end users and developers to interact with the protocol with confidence.

Lending Program

The core Solend program is deployed on Solana mainnet and handles all supply, borrow, repay, and withdraw operations. Written in Rust using the Anchor framework, the program has been audited multiple times by leading security firms. It implements strict access controls, overflow protection, and reentrancy guards at every entry point.

Isolated Lending Pools

Solend operates multiple isolated lending pools in addition to the main pool. Each isolated pool has its own risk parameters, supported assets, and debt ceilings. Isolation prevents contagion — a bad debt event in one isolated pool cannot impact the main pool or other isolated pools. This architecture allows the protocol to support higher-risk, long-tail assets safely.

Oracle System

Solend uses Pyth Network as its primary price oracle, with Switchboard as a secondary fallback. Pyth provides high-frequency, low-latency price feeds sourced directly from institutional market makers, making it ideal for on-chain DeFi applications. Price staleness checks and circuit breakers protect the protocol from oracle manipulation and flash loan price attacks.

Why Solana?

Solend was built on Solana from day one. The technical capabilities of the Solana blockchain are uniquely suited to the demands of a high-performance lending protocol used by hundreds of thousands of participants.

<1s

Sub-Second Finality

Solana achieves transaction finality in under 400 milliseconds — over 100x faster than Ethereum. For a lending protocol, this means liquidations happen faster, reducing risk. Users can adjust positions and react to market movements in near real-time, making Solend practical for active portfolio management strategies that would be cost-prohibitive on slower chains.

$0.001

Near-Zero Transaction Costs

Average transaction fees on Solana are approximately $0.001, compared to $5–$50+ on Ethereum during peak periods. Low fees make Solend accessible to users of all portfolio sizes — not just whales. Smaller accounts can supply modest amounts and still earn meaningful yields without fee costs eroding their returns. This democratizes access to DeFi primitives for a global user base.

65K TPS

Massive Throughput Capacity

Solana's theoretical maximum throughput of 65,000 transactions per second ensures the network never becomes congested under normal DeFi activity. For Solend, this means the liquidation engine can process thousands of positions simultaneously during market volatility events — a critical safety feature that protects lenders and the overall protocol from bad debt accumulation.

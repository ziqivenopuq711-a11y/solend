Tokenomics

SLND Token

SLND is the governance and utility token of the Solend protocol. SLND holders have the power to propose and vote on protocol upgrades, parameter changes, treasury allocations, and new market listings. Beyond governance, SLND provides tangible utility through fee discounts, liquidity mining rewards, and staking for protocol revenue sharing. Total supply is fixed at 100,000,000 SLND — no inflation, no minting after genesis.

Token Distribution

The SLND token distribution was designed to prioritize community ownership while ensuring adequate resources for long-term protocol development and sustainable growth.

Token Utility

SLND is not just a governance token — it provides real, tangible benefits to holders who actively participate in the Solend ecosystem.

🗳️

Governance Voting

1 SLND = 1 vote on all protocol governance proposals. Vote on new asset listings, risk parameter changes, treasury allocations, protocol upgrades, and strategic partnerships.

💸

Fee Discounts

SLND holders who stake their tokens receive discounts on protocol fees. Staked SLND tiers provide 10%, 25%, and 50% discounts on flash loan fees and other protocol charges.

⛏️

Liquidity Mining

Users who supply and borrow assets on Solend earn SLND rewards in addition to standard interest rates. Rewards are distributed proportionally based on market share and boost multipliers.

🏦

Revenue Share

Staked SLND holders receive a portion of protocol revenue generated from the protocol fee (a percentage of interest paid by borrowers) and flash loan fees accumulated by the treasury.

Liquidity Mining Program

Solend distributes SLND tokens to users who supply and borrow assets on the protocol. This liquidity mining program incentivizes growth and rewards early and active participants. Rewards are calculated per block and claimed by users at any time with no lock-up on claimed tokens.

The emission rate follows a halving schedule: emissions are reduced by 50% approximately every 12 months, creating a predictable and deflationary distribution curve. This ensures sustainable rewards that don't dilute holders excessively over time, while still incentivizing liquidity provision during the protocol's growth phase.

Different markets receive different shares of the total emissions, with allocations voted on by governance quarterly. Higher-priority markets like the main USDC and SOL pools typically receive the largest allocations, while newer isolated markets may receive bonus multipliers to bootstrap initial liquidity.

Staking & Revenue Share

By staking SLND, token holders participate directly in protocol economics. Stakers earn a share of all fees generated by the Solend protocol — creating a strong incentive to hold and actively participate in governance.

Protocol Fee Sources

The Solend protocol collects a spread between borrow and supply rates (typically 15–20% of interest) as protocol revenue. Flash loan fees of 0.09% also flow to the treasury. A portion of this revenue (voted on by governance) is distributed to SLND stakers as a direct reward for securing and governing the protocol.

Staking Mechanism

Users stake SLND to receive xSLND, a receipt token representing their staked position plus accumulated rewards. The xSLND:SLND exchange rate increases over time as protocol revenue is added to the staking pool. Unstaking has a 7-day unbonding period, protecting the protocol from governance attacks and ensuring long-term alignment among stakers.

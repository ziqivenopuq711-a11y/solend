Governance

Decentralized Governance

Solend is governed by its community of SLND token holders. Every significant protocol decision — from risk parameter adjustments and new asset listings to treasury allocations and strategic partnerships — is decided through transparent, on-chain governance votes. No single entity controls Solend; the community collectively owns and steers the protocol's future.

SLND Voting Power

Governance on Solend follows a simple and transparent model: 1 SLND = 1 vote. Any wallet holding SLND can participate in governance votes directly, or delegate their voting power to another wallet — such as a trusted community member, a protocol that holds SLND, or a governance specialist who actively researches proposals.

For a proposal to pass, it must meet a minimum quorum of 1,000,000 SLND tokens participating in the vote. This threshold ensures that major protocol changes reflect broad community consensus rather than a small group of voters. The quorum threshold itself can be changed through governance with sufficient community support.

The standard voting period is 3 days (72 hours), giving the community adequate time to review, discuss, and vote on proposals. After a proposal passes, a 2-day timelock delay is enforced before execution, allowing time for users to act if they disagree with the outcome — for example, withdrawing funds before a contentious parameter change takes effect.

Proposal Process

Solend uses a staged governance process that ensures community input at every step, from initial idea to on-chain execution. Anyone can start this process — no minimum SLND holding is required to discuss or submit a temperature check.

1

Temperature Check

Post your idea in the #governance channel on Discord. Gather informal feedback from community members to gauge interest and refine the concept before investing time in a full proposal write-up. This stage is casual and exploratory — no formal structure required.

2

Forum Discussion

Create a structured post on the Solend governance forum with a full proposal specification: motivation, technical details, risk analysis, and expected outcomes. The forum post should remain open for at least 5 days for thorough community discussion and revision before proceeding to an on-chain vote.

3

On-Chain Vote

Submit the proposal on-chain using Solend's governance contract. Any wallet holding at least 100,000 SLND can submit a proposal. The voting period lasts 3 days. Votes are final and verifiable on-chain. The proposal passes if quorum is reached and the majority votes in favor.

4

Timelock Execution

After passing, the proposal enters a 2-day timelock queue managed by the governance multisig. During this window, the community can review the exact transactions to be executed. After the timelock expires, any holder can trigger execution. Emergency pause can halt execution if a critical flaw is discovered.

DAO Structure & Treasury

The Solend DAO is the collective body of SLND token holders. The DAO controls the community treasury — currently holding approximately 42 million SLND tokens plus USDC and other assets accumulated from protocol fees. All treasury transactions require successful governance proposals to execute.

A 5-of-9 multisig guards the treasury and acts as the final execution layer for passed proposals. Multisig signers are elected by governance and include core team members, respected community contributors, and ecosystem partners. The multisig can veto a proposal execution only in cases of clear technical errors or security threats, not for political disagreement.

Treasury funds have been deployed to protocol audits, liquidity mining programs, developer grants, ecosystem partnerships, conference sponsorships, and operational costs. The DAO publishes a quarterly treasury report on the governance forum for full transparency.

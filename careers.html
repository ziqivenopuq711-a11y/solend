How We Work

Solend is a remote-first team distributed across multiple time zones — from San Francisco to Singapore. We operate asynchronously by default, using written communication to make decisions, share context, and collaborate across time zones without requiring overlapping hours. Our Notion workspace and GitHub serve as our single source of truth.

We have a strong engineering culture rooted in correctness and security. Before any code ships to production, it goes through peer review, internal security review, and often external audit. We'd rather ship slower and get it right than move fast and break things — because "things" in our case means real user funds.

We communicate clearly, give direct feedback, and debate ideas vigorously but respectfully. Everyone on the team is expected to contribute to protocol direction, not just their immediate domain. The best idea wins regardless of who proposes it — seniority is earned through the quality of your work and thinking, not years at the company.

How to Apply

We have a streamlined hiring process designed to be respectful of your time while ensuring we find the right fit for both you and the team. The process typically takes 2–3 weeks from application to offer.

1

Application

Send your resume and a short note on why you want to join Solend to careers@solend.fi. Include links to relevant work — GitHub, deployed contracts, or projects you're proud of.

2

Intro Call

30-minute video call with the hiring manager to discuss the role, your background, and what you're looking for. We'll also answer any questions you have about the team and protocol.

3

Technical Review

A take-home technical assignment or in-depth technical discussion depending on the role. We aim to make this representative of real work you'd do at Solend, not abstract puzzles.

4

Team Interviews

Meet 2–3 team members across different functions. We evaluate technical skills, problem-solving approach, and cultural fit. Offer and onboarding follow within a week of final interviews.

Features

Powerful DeFi Primitives

Solend provides a comprehensive set of DeFi building blocks designed for speed, capital efficiency, and composability. Whether you're a retail user looking to earn yield, a developer building automated strategies, or an institution managing large positions, Solend's feature set meets your needs with the security and reliability required at scale.

Core Feature

Lending Pools & Dynamic APYs

Users can supply liquidity to Solend's main lending pool to earn dynamic interest rates determined entirely by market utilization. The higher the demand for borrowing a given asset, the higher the supply APY for that asset — creating an automatic yield maximization mechanism that responds to real market conditions in real time.

The protocol currently supports over 15 assets including SOL, USDC, USDT, ETH (Wormhole), BTC (Wormhole), mSOL, stSOL, BONK, JitoSOL, and more. Interest compounds continuously at the block level, meaning your earnings grow with every slot produced on Solana — approximately every 400 milliseconds. This continuous compounding maximizes yield efficiency compared to daily or weekly compounding structures.

When you supply to Solend, you receive cTokens (collateral tokens) representing your share of the pool. These cTokens can themselves be used as collateral for borrowing, enabling capital-efficient looping strategies for advanced users seeking to maximize yield exposure.

Advanced Feature

Flash Loans

Flash loans are uncollateralized loans that must be borrowed and repaid within a single Solana transaction. If the repayment does not occur, the entire transaction reverts as if it never happened — making flash loans completely risk-free for the protocol. Solend charges a fixed fee of 0.09% on all flash loan amounts, which is distributed to the protocol treasury and liquidity providers.

Primary use cases include:

  • ▸ Arbitrage between DEXes (Jupiter, Orca, Raydium) without upfront capital
  • ▸ Collateral swaps — replace one collateral type with another atomically
  • ▸ Self-liquidation to avoid penalty fees from third-party liquidators
  • ▸ Leverage amplification in a single atomic transaction
Risk Architecture

Isolated Markets

Solend's isolated market architecture allows the protocol to support a wider range of assets while protecting core liquidity providers from long-tail asset risk.

Main Pool

The main pool supports blue-chip assets like SOL, USDC, ETH, and BTC with the highest liquidity and lowest risk parameters. This pool contains the majority of Solend's TVL and serves as the primary liquidity source for large institutional borrowers and yield farmers seeking the most stable and liquid markets on the protocol.

Isolated Pools

Long-tail assets with lower liquidity or higher volatility are listed in isolated pools. Each isolated pool has its own debt ceiling, collateral factors, and risk parameters tuned specifically for those assets. Losses in an isolated pool cannot propagate to the main pool, ensuring that a single asset's failure does not threaten the entire protocol's solvency.

Custom Pools

Protocols and DAOs can request custom isolated pools with bespoke parameters for their specific use case — such as a yield strategy vault that needs to borrow against a specific liquidity provider token. Custom pools enable deep protocol composability while maintaining strict risk isolation boundaries between different market segments and user cohorts.

Protocol Safety

Risk Management & Liquidations

Solend's automated liquidation engine is the backbone of protocol solvency. When a borrower's health factor drops below 1.0 — meaning their borrow value exceeds their collateral value times the liquidation threshold — their position becomes eligible for liquidation by any participant in the network.

Liquidators are economically incentivized through a liquidation bonus, which is a discount on the collateral they receive. For most assets, this bonus is 5%, meaning liquidators can repay 50% of a borrower's outstanding debt and receive collateral worth 105% of what they paid. This creates a competitive market for liquidations that keeps the protocol healthy even during rapid market downturns.

Solend also maintains a protocol-owned insurance fund, built up from a portion of protocol fees, that acts as a backstop in extreme scenarios where liquidations cannot fully cover bad debt. This multi-layer risk management system has maintained full solvency since the protocol's launch in September 2021.

Safe
